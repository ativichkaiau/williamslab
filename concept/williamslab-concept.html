R&D garage & telemetry center for research

Why research needs active suspension

A race car with a passive chassis pitches, rolls, and floats over kerbs — it loses tyre contact at the exact instant it needs grip, so the driver can't commit. Active suspension senses load in real time and counter-actuates to keep the chassis flat and all four tyres planted.

A research project has the identical failure mode. Every source of uncertainty — a vague hypothesis, a broken mechanistic link, a missing control, an underpowered n — makes the project pitch and lose grip on reality. Commit budget and months into that instability and you crash: unpublishable, unfundable, irreproducible.

What WilliamsLab actuates

WilliamsLab is the active-suspension controller for the research chassis. It reads telemetry across the full load path —

HYPOTHESIS → MECHANISM → ASSAY → COHORT → DATA → STATISTICS → STORY

— and the moment a subsystem starts to pitch, it flags the instability and calls the counter-actuation (the repair) before you turn in. Grip is your contact with evidence. Downforce is statistical power. The pit wall reads the numbers so you can drive the science, not fight the car.

05 · RESEARCH ACTIVE SUSPENSION

Nine instability sensors

Each sensor watches one way a research chassis loses grip. When it trips, WilliamsLab shows the warning sign, calls the counter-actuation, and points at the exact place in your Brugada program to apply it.

InstabilityWarning sign (telemetry)Repair (counter-actuation)Brugada example
Unclear hypothesis No falsification criterion; no predicted direction or magnitude. Rewrite as if/then with direction + effect size + the observation that would kill it. "Epigenetics affects Brugada" → "SCN5A-promoter methylation is higher in spontaneous type-1 and predicts ≥25% lower Nav1.5 mRNA."
Weak mechanistic chain A causal edge with no assay behind it — a "miracle step." Insert an intermediate measurement, or downgrade the edge to correlational until bridged. Methylation → arrhythmia skips INa and conduction; add iPSC-CM patch clamp + MEA to bridge the gap.
Missing control group A comparison with no proper reference or input track. Add matched / isogenic controls and assay-level inputs (IgG, spike-ins). Need SCN5A-negative healthy controls, a spontaneous-vs-drug-induced split, and isogenic gene-corrected iPSC-CM.
Assay mismatch The assay can't measure the claimed variable — wrong cell type or resolution. Swap the assay or the model; validate cross-tissue concordance. Using blood methylation to claim cardiomyocyte state → move to iPSC-CM / cardiac tissue, or prove blood–heart concordance first.
Underpowered design n too small to survive genome-wide FDR for the expected effect. Run the power calc; make a targeted locus the primary endpoint; pool or expand n. Genome-wide RRBS at n=6 is underpowered → candidate-locus pyroseq is the primary; RRBS is hypothesis-generating discovery.
Literature gap A load-bearing claim is unsupported, contradicted, or unknowingly redundant. Deep-scan with Literature Radar; reposition the novelty explicitly. Confirm whether SCN10A-enhancer methylation in Brugada is already published; position against existing SCN5A-methylation reports.
Statistical ambiguity No pre-specified primary endpoint; flexible analysis; uncontrolled confounders. Pre-register; fix primary/secondary; model covariates; correct multiple testing; use mediation for causal claims. Na⁺-blocker medication confounds methylation — adjust as covariate; test mediation methylation → expression → QRS.
Infeasible protocol Timeline, samples, skills, or equipment exceed reality. De-scope and phase; recruit a core facility / collaborator; use a surrogate model. 4C + CUT&Tag + iPSC-CM patch clamp in 12 months with one tech is infeasible → phase Y1 methylation/expression, Y2 chromatin, Y3 function.
Manuscript-story weakness Results don't deliver the title's promise; reviewer will ask "so what / is it causal / is it cardiac?" Align title → figures → claim; add the single experiment that converts association to mechanism; pre-empt objections. Association-only draft is weak → add an iPSC-CM demethylation / rescue experiment and frame clinical utility via the biomarker score.

08 · MVP · THE SHAKEDOWN CAR

What to build first

A v1 you can ship in weeks, not seasons. It is a thinking-and-planning cockpit over one shared graph — not a data-processing backend. The signature feature (active suspension) ships in v1; heavy compute does not.

✅ Must-have (v1)

  • Project graph — nodes + typed edges, one project (Brugada).
  • Hypothesis Graph + Mechanism Map — the core differentiator.
  • Literature Radar (manual) — PMID/DOI import, tagging, gap notes.
  • Active-Suspension checklist — rule-based flags for the 9 instabilities.
  • Assay↔claim matrix — link each edge to a planned assay.
  • The Pit Wall dashboard — the Brugada telemetry view.

◇ Nice-to-have (v1.x)

  • Auto literature ingestion via the PubMed E-utilities API.
  • Data-pipeline DAG templates per assay type.
  • Power / sample-size calculators in the Stats Board.
  • Figure-plan & IMRaD scaffold export.
  • LLM-assisted hypothesis critique & edge-evidence grading.
  • Multi-project + collaborator roles.

⊘ Avoid in v1

  • Running real bioinformatics / compute (no FASTQ crunching).
  • Full ELN / LIMS sample-tracking features.
  • Automated ethics/IRB filing.
  • Real-time ML literature ranking at scale.
  • Mobile apps and native clients.
  • Anything that isn't the graph, the sensors, or the dashboard.
